## Every new patient starts with a search. Are they finding you?

When someone moves to Sandy and needs a dentist, they don't ask their new neighbor. They search. "Dentist near me." "Best dentist in Sandy." "Cosmetic dentist Salt Lake City." And increasingly: "Hey ChatGPT, who's a good dentist near me?"

The practice that appears in those results gets the call. The practice that doesn't never knows the patient existed.

Utah is the 4th fastest-growing state in the country — 7.1% population growth between 2020-2024. Every one of those new residents needs a dentist. The practices capturing those patients are the ones showing up in search. The practices losing them are the ones relying on word-of-mouth and hoping the phone rings.

## How patients actually choose a dentist in 2026

The patient journey has three stages, and most dental practices only optimize for the last one.

### Stage 1: Problem search

The patient has a symptom, not a dentist in mind:
- "tooth pain won't go away"
- "chipped tooth what to do"
- "is invisalign worth it"
- "wisdom teeth removal cost"
- "do I need a root canal"

Practices that publish helpful content answering these questions capture patients at the very beginning of their journey. By the time the patient is ready to book, they've already read your content — you're the default choice.

Most dental websites skip this stage entirely. They list services but don't answer questions.

### Stage 2: Provider search

The patient knows they need a dentist and searches for one:
- "dentist near me"
- "best dentist salt lake city"
- "cosmetic dentist draper"
- "emergency dentist sandy utah"
- "pediatric dentist lehi"

This is where the Google Local Pack (the map results) dominates. The three practices shown on the map get the majority of clicks. If your practice isn't in the Local Pack for these searches, you're losing patients daily.

### Stage 3: Evaluation

The patient has 2-3 names. Now they decide:
- **Reviews** — 71% of patients read reviews before choosing. Volume, recency, rating, and whether the dentist responds to reviews all matter.
- **Website** — Does it look professional? Can they find the specific service they need? Is there online scheduling?
- **AI check** — A growing number of patients ask ChatGPT or Perplexity: "Is [practice name] a good dentist?" or "Who's the best dentist for implants in Salt Lake City?"

This stage is where most practices lose patients without knowing it. The patient visited your website, read your reviews, and chose someone else — you never got the call.

## The six pillars of dental SEO

### 1. Google Business Profile (the foundation)

Your GBP is the single most important dental SEO asset. When someone searches "dentist near me," Google shows the Local Pack — and your GBP determines whether you appear.

**Optimize completely:**
- Primary category: "Dentist" or "Cosmetic dentist"
- Secondary categories: add all that apply (pediatric, orthodontist, oral surgeon, etc.)
- Services: list every service with descriptions
- Hours: include emergency availability
- Photos: office, team, before/after (with consent), equipment — add 2-3 new photos per week
- Posts: weekly updates (tips, promotions, team highlights, community involvement)
- Q&A: answer every question; proactively add common questions

**Respond to every review within 24 hours.** Thank positive reviewers by name and mention the procedure. For negative reviews, acknowledge the concern and offer to resolve it offline. Google measures response rate and speed.

### 2. Service-specific pages

This is where most dental websites fail. A typical site has an "Our Services" page listing 15 procedures in bullet points. Google can't rank a bullet point.

Create a dedicated page for every major service:
- General dentistry / cleanings and exams
- Dental implants
- Cosmetic dentistry / veneers
- Invisalign / orthodontics
- Teeth whitening
- Root canal / endodontics
- Wisdom teeth removal
- Emergency dental care
- Pediatric dentistry
- Sedation dentistry

Each page should include: what the procedure involves, who it's for, what to expect during and after, approximate recovery time, and — if appropriate — general pricing guidance. Target specific keywords: "dental implants Salt Lake City," "Invisalign draper utah," "emergency dentist sandy."

### 3. Review generation

Reviews are the primary trust signal for dental practices — both for Google rankings and AI recommendations.

**Build a systematic review process:**
- Post-appointment text or email with a direct Google review link (not your website — Google directly)
- QR card at the front desk with "We'd appreciate your feedback"
- Follow-up after major procedures (implants, cosmetic work) when the patient sees the result
- One reminder after 3 days if no review — no more, don't be pushy

**Target metrics:**
- 5+ new reviews per month (consistency matters more than bursts)
- 4.7+ star average
- Response to 100% of reviews within 24 hours
- Reviews mentioning specific procedures ("great experience with my implants") provide keyword signals

### 4. Local content

Content that includes Utah and neighborhood context ranks locally while national dental sites rank nationally.

**High-value content ideas for Utah dental practices:**
- "How Much Do Dental Implants Cost in Salt Lake City?"
- "Finding a Pediatric Dentist in Draper: What Parents Should Know"
- "Is Invisalign or Traditional Braces Better? A Utah Dentist's Perspective"
- "Emergency Dental Care in Sandy: What to Do When You Can't Wait"
- "Water Quality and Dental Health in Utah: What Patients Should Know"
- "Sports Mouthguards for Utah Youth Athletes: A Guide for Parents"

The Utah-specific angle makes this content relevant locally while being unique enough that national sites don't compete for the same keywords.

### 5. Schema markup

Structured data tells Google (and AI) exactly what your practice offers. For dental practices, implement:

- **Dentist** schema — practice name, address, phone, hours, accepted insurance
- **MedicalProcedure** schema — for major procedures (implants, Invisalign, etc.)
- **FAQPage** schema — for patient question content
- **Review/AggregateRating** schema — your review scores
- **LocalBusiness** schema — for each office location

Schema markup is invisible to patients but directly feeds Google's rich results (the enhanced listings with ratings, hours, and FAQ answers) and AI recommendation systems.

### 6. AI search optimization

When a relocating family asks ChatGPT "who's a good family dentist in Sandy?" — does your practice appear?

This is the fastest-growing channel for new patient acquisition, and most dental practices are completely invisible to it.

**What drives AI dental recommendations:**
- Consistent entity data across all platforms (Google, Healthgrades, Yelp, Zocdoc, your website)
- Strong review profile (volume, recency, and ratings across multiple platforms)
- Structured content with direct answers to patient questions
- Professional authority signals (association memberships, publications, credentials)
- Practice mentioned on authoritative sources (local news, dental directories, community pages)

## Multi-location considerations

For dental practices with multiple locations, each location needs:
- Its own Google Business Profile, fully optimized
- Its own landing page on the website with unique content (not copy-pasted with the city name swapped)
- Location-specific review management
- Consistent NAP across all directories for each location separately
- Individual structured data markup per location

NAP conflicts between locations — slightly different phone formats, abbreviated vs. full street names — confuse both Google and AI. Audit and standardize every listing for every location.

All of them. Google is the most important for Local Pack visibility, but AI recommendation systems pull from multiple sources.
